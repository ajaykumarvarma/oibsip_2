Time Line of Sundar Pichai

  • 1970 - Born in Madurai,India.

  • Education - Pichai earned his degree from the Indian Institute of Technology Kharagpur in metallurgical engineering

  • Career - Pichai worked in engineering and product management at Applied Materials and in management consulting at McKinsey & Company
  • Pichai joined Google in 2004, where he led the product management and innovation efforts for a suite of Google's client software products, including Google Chrome and Chrome OS, as well as being largely responsible for Google Drive.

  • He went on to oversee the development of other applications such as Gmail and Google Maps.

  • 2009 - Pichai gave a demonstration of Chrome OS; the Chromebook was released for trial and testing in 2011, and released to the public in 2012.

  • 2010 -he announced the open-sourcing of the new video codec VP8 by Google and introduced the new video format, WebM

  • 2013- Pichai added Android to the list of Google products that he oversaw. Android was formerly managed by Andy Rubin.[23] He was a director of Jive Software from April 2011 to July 30, 2013

  • 2015 - Pichai was selected to become the next CEO of Google on August 10, 2015 after previously being appointed Product Chief by CEO, Larry Page. On October 24, 2015 he stepped into the new position at the completion of the formation of Alphabet Inc., the new holding company for the Google company family

His Success at Google

Sundar Pichai's interview at Google coincidently coincided with the launch of Google Gmail. Unaware of the new product, Pichai admitted his ignorance during his interviews and is remembered as the candidate with "intellectual humility," which Laszlo Bock, Google’s former senior VP of people operations, called one of the top qualities he looked for in candidates at Google. Pichai was well-prepared but managed a dynamic situation with candor during a moment of uncertainty



On April 1, 2004, Sundar Pichai joined Google as its vice-president of product management and was tasked with the development of Google Toolbar and Google Chrome, which grew to become the world’s most popular internet browser.



Pichai launched the Chrome OS and Chromebook in 2011, headed the Android operating system in 2013, and became the leader of all products and engineering at Google by 2014. Pichai oversaw all of Google’s products and platforms, including Search, Maps, Play, Android, Chrome, Gmail, and Google Workspace.



Under Pichai's leadership, Google invested in new opportunities such as Google Cloud and YouTube and continues to research advanced technologies, including machine learning and quantum computing. In 2021, Sundar Pichai stated that artificial intelligence, when machines are programmed to simulate the human mind and solve problems and mimic human activity, "will have a bigger impact than most of the breakthroughs in recent history" and "can make humans more productive than we ever imagined." Pichai expects AI to play a foundational role in every aspect of our lives, by mimicking human tasks, from healthcare and education to how we manufacture things and consume information.



In 2021, Alphabet crossed $2 trillion in market value due to sales and profit growth during the COVID-19 pandemic. While many other technology giants embrace the metaverse as the next frontier of growth, Sundar Pichai sees Google’s future in its oldest offering, the Internet search, with consumers asking computers more questions with voice and multimodal experiences.
